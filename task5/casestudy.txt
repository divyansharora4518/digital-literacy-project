                                                  UPI Payment Fraud Case Study
UPI payment fraud is a common cybercrime where scammers trick people into sending money through digital payment apps. In this case, a college student named Rahul received a phone call from a person pretending to be a bank official. The caller informed him that his bank account would be blocked unless he verified his details.
First, the scammer gained Rahul’s trust by using official language. Then, he asked Rahul to open his UPI app and approve a “verification request.” Rahul believed it was necessary and entered his UPI PIN. Within seconds, ₹10,000 was deducted from his account.
Such frauds usually target students, elderly people, and those who are not very familiar with digital payments. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online services.
This case highlights the importance of never sharing UPI PINs or approving unknown requests. Banks never ask for sensitive details over phone calls.
